# 📊 E-Commerce Sales & Customer Analytics using SQL

## 🎯 Objective
The goal of this project is to analyze e-commerce sales data and extract meaningful business insights related to revenue, customer behavior, product performance, and sales trends using SQL.

---

## 🛠 Tools & Technologies
- MySQL
- SQL (Aggregations, Group By, Window Functions, Date Functions)

---

## 📂 Dataset Description
The dataset contains transactional sales data including:
- Order ID
- Order Date
- Customer ID
- Region
- Product Category
- Quantity
- Unit Price
- Discount
- Revenue
- Payment Method
- Delivery Days
- Customer Rating

---

## 🧱 Schema Design
A normalized relational schema was designed from the flat dataset including:
- Customers
- Orders
- Products
- Order Details

(ER Diagram attached in project files)

---

## 📊 Key Analysis Performed
- Total Revenue, Orders, and Customers
- Revenue by Region
- Product Category Performance
- Customer Spending Analysis
- Payment Method Trends
- Monthly Revenue Trends
- Customer Ranking using Window Functions

---

## 💡 Key Insights

- The West region generates the highest revenue, indicating strong market demand and better    performance compared to other regions.
- Electronics is the top-performing product category, contributing the largest share of total revenue.
- Revenue shows a steady upward trend over time, indicating consistent business growth.
- UPI is the most preferred payment method, reflecting a shift towards digital transactions.
- A small group of customers contributes significantly to total revenue, highlighting the importance of high-value customers.
